Caramelized Onion and Goat Cheese Tartlets

  • 2 large onions, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • 6 oz goat cheese, crumbled
  • 4 tbsp balsamic glaze
  • 2 tsp sea salt
  • Fresh thyme for garnish
  1. Caramelize the Onions: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and cook gently until softened and caramelized, stirring frequently for about 15 to 20 minutes. Once cooked, set the caramelized onions aside to cool.
  2. Prepare the Tartlets: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the puff pastry and cut it into equal squares. Place the pastry squares on a parchmentlined baking tray. Top each pastry square with a spoonful of the caramelized onions, leaving a small border around the edges. Bake in the preheated oven for about 15 minutes or until the pastry is golden and puffed.
  3. Assemble the Tartlets: Remove the baked pastry from the oven. Crumble the goat cheese over the tartlets and sprinkle with fresh thyme leaves. Drizzle the tartlets with balsamic glaze and a sprinkle of sea salt.
  4. Allow the tartlets to cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with additional fresh thyme, if desired. Serve the caramelized onion and goat cheese tartlets as a delicious appetizer or snack.
