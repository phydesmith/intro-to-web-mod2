Savory Spinach and Feta Stuffed Chicken Breasts
### Ingredients
 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
 6 oz spinach, chopped
 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
 3 tablespoons cream cheese
 1/4 cup chopped scallions
 2 cloves garlic, minced
 3 tablespoons olive oil
 Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 450°F.
2. In a medium bowl, mix the chopped spinach, feta cheese, cream cheese, scallions, and minced garlic.
3. Cut a horizontal slit through the thickest part of each chicken breast, creating a pocket for the filling.
4. Divide the spinach and feta mixture evenly and stuff it into the pockets of the chicken breasts.
5. Season the outside of the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
6. In an ovenproof skillet, heat the olive oil over mediumhigh heat.
7. Brown the stuffed chicken breasts for about 4 minutes on each side.
8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 57 minutes or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F.

Serve the savory spinach and feta stuffed chicken breasts with your favorite sides for a delicious and impressive meal.

This recipe offers a simple yet flavorful way to prepare stuffed chicken breasts, combining the richness of feta cheese with the freshness of spinach to create a satisfying main course[1][2][3].

Caramelized Onion and Goat Cheese Tartlets
### Ingredients
 2 large onions, peeled and thinly sliced
 4 tbsp butter
 1 sheet puff pastry
 6 oz goat cheese, crumbled
 4 tbsp balsamic glaze
 2 tsp sea salt
 Fresh thyme for garnish

### Instructions
1. Caramelize the Onions:
    In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
    Add the thinly sliced onions and cook gently until softened and caramelized, stirring frequently for about 15 to 20 minutes.
    Once cooked, set the caramelized onions aside to cool.

2. Prepare the Tartlets:
    Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    Roll out the puff pastry and cut it into equal squares. Place the pastry squares on a parchmentlined baking tray.
    Top each pastry square with a spoonful of the caramelized onions, leaving a small border around the edges.
    Bake in the preheated oven for about 15 minutes or until the pastry is golden and puffed.

3. Assemble the Tartlets:
    Remove the baked pastry from the oven.
    Crumble the goat cheese over the tartlets and sprinkle with fresh thyme leaves.
    Drizzle the tartlets with balsamic glaze and a sprinkle of sea salt.

4. Serve:
    Allow the tartlets to cool for a few minutes before serving.
    Garnish with additional fresh thyme, if desired.
    Serve the caramelized onion and goat cheese tartlets as a delicious appetizer or snack.

This recipe yields delightful Caramelized Onion and Goat Cheese Tartlets, featuring the sweet, savory flavors of caramelized onions paired with creamy goat cheese on a crispy puff pastry base[1][2].

Spicy Mango and Black Bean Quinoa Salad
### Ingredients
 1 cup quinoa
 2 cups water or vegetable broth
 1 (15ounce) can black beans, drained and rinsed
 1 large mango, diced
 1 red bell pepper, diced
 4 green onions, thinly sliced
 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
 1/2 cup pepitas (pumpkin seeds), toasted
 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
 1 teaspoon chipotle chili powder
 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
 Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions
1. Cook the Quinoa:
    Rinse the quinoa under running water. In a medium saucepan, bring the water or vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and allow it to cool.

2. Prepare the Salad:
    In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, diced mango, red bell pepper, green onions, and chopped cilantro.

3. Make the Dressing:
    In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, fresh lime juice, honey or maple syrup, chipotle chili powder, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.

4. Assemble the Salad:
    Pour the dressing over the quinoa mixture and toss to combine. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

5. Serve:
    Garnish the salad with toasted pepitas just before serving.
    This salad can be served immediately or chilled for a few hours to allow the flavors to meld.

This Spicy Mango and Black Bean Quinoa Salad is a vibrant and satisfying dish that combines the sweetness of mango with the earthy flavor of black beans and the nuttiness of quinoa. The chipotlelime dressing adds a zesty and spicy kick to the salad, making it a perfect side dish or light meal[1][2][3][4].

Decadent Dark Chocolate Raspberry Brownies
### Ingredients
 200g dark chocolate, broken into chunks
 100g milk chocolate, broken into chunks
 250g salted butter
 400g soft light brown sugar
 4 large eggs
 140g plain flour
 50g cocoa powder
 200g fresh raspberries
 100g chocolate chips

### Instructions
1. Preparation:
    Preheat the oven to 180°C (160°C fan) and line a 20 x 30cm baking tray with parchment paper.

2. Melt the Chocolate and Butter:
    In a heatproof bowl, melt the dark chocolate, milk chocolate, and butter together, stirring occasionally. Allow the mixture to cool slightly.

3. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients:
    Stir the brown sugar into the chocolate mixture.
    Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
    Sift in the flour and cocoa powder, and gently fold until just combined.

4. Add Raspberries and Chocolate Chips:
    Gently fold in the fresh raspberries and chocolate chips until evenly distributed.

5. Bake:
    Pour the brownie batter into the prepared tray and smooth the top.
    Bake in the preheated oven for 3035 minutes or until the brownies are set around the edges but still slightly soft in the middle.

6. Cool and Serve:
    Allow the brownies to cool completely in the tray before cutting into squares.

These Decadent Dark Chocolate Raspberry Brownies are a delightful combination of rich, fudgy chocolate and bursts of sweet, tart raspberries, making them a perfect indulgence for any occasion[2][4][5].
